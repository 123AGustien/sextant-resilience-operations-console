# 🏦 Institutional Overview — Sextant Resilience Platform

## 1. Executive Summary
The Sextant Resilience Platform is a system-of-systems designed to model, simulate, and analyze infrastructure resilience under real-world degradation conditions.

It enables institutions to observe how systems behave before failure occurs, not only after failure has happened.

---

## 2. Problem Statement
Modern critical infrastructure systems:
- Fail in cascading patterns
- Degrade before failure is visible
- Lack structured dependency observability
- Are reactive instead of predictive

---

## 3. Solution Approach
This platform introduces:

- Real-time resilience simulation
- Dependency-aware failure propagation modelling
- Continuous degradation monitoring
- Scenario-based stress testing

---

## 4. Key Capabilities

### 4.1 Resilience Simulation Engine
Models system behavior under stress and failure conditions.

### 4.2 Dependency Mapping
Tracks how systems influence each other during degradation.

### 4.3 Failure Propagation Analysis
Simulates cascading failure across connected systems.

### 4.4 Audit-Ready Logging
Maintains structured event traces for institutional review.

---

## 5. Institutional Use Cases

- Financial infrastructure risk modelling
- Critical system resilience validation
- Regulatory stress testing alignment
- Operational continuity planning

---

## 6. Governance Alignment (Conceptual)

This system is designed to support:
- MAS-style operational resilience expectations
- IMDA digital infrastructure evaluation
- Enterprise risk management frameworks

---

## 7. Core Principle

> Resilience is not the absence of failure — it is the controlled observability of degradation before failure occurs.

---

## 8. Conclusion
The platform provides a structured environment for understanding, simulating, and preparing for system failure in complex infrastructures.
